Important Warning

Tantra Safety &
Responsible Practice

The ancient fire warms. It also burns. Know how to approach.

The First Rule of Tantra

"Tantra without Guru is poison.
Tantra with Guru is nectar."

Never practice tantra without a living, initiated Guru. Not from YouTube. Not from books alone. Not from online courses without live transmission. Not from experimenting with mantras you found on the internet. There is no exception to this rule.

Dangers of Unsupervised Practice

Danger
Kundalini Sickness

Premature or uncontrolled awakening can cause psychosis, chronic pain, insomnia, and mental instability.

Danger
Energy Imbalance

Incorrect mantra pronunciation or visualization can create blockages, causing depression, rage, or physical illness.

Danger
Entity Attachment

Certain practices open portals. Without protection, negative entities may attach to the practitioner.

Danger
Mantra Backfire

Some mantras rebound on the practitioner if done incorrectly.

Danger
Psychological Fragmentation

Intense sadhana without grounding can dissolve the ego prematurely, leading to derealization.

Danger
Physical Harm

Some practices involve specific body postures or breath retention that can harm if done wrong.

Signs of Unsafe Practice

Seek immediate guidance if you experience: unexplained rage, terror, or despair after practice; persistent headaches, chest pain, or burning sensations; hearing voices or seeing entities outside meditation; inability to sleep for days; loss of boundaries between self and others; sudden life collapse; or feeling of being watched. Do not ignore these signs. Contact a qualified Guru immediately.

If something goes wrong, stop all practice immediately and call the ashram.

Who Should NOT Practice Tantra

You should NOT practice if you have untreated psychosis, schizophrenia, or bipolar disorder; are currently suicidal or experiencing severe depression; are under the influence of drugs or alcohol; are seeking quick power for ego reasons; cannot commit to regular practice; or are unwilling to follow a Guru.
